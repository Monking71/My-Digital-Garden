*For positive integers $\color{blue} k$ and $\color{blue} n$, the number of $\color{blue} k$-combinations with unlimited repetition of a set of $\color{blue} n$ objects is $\color{blue} C(k+n-1,k)=\frac{(k+n-1)!}{k!(n-1)!}$*
